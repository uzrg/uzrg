> I'm a Senior Systems Engineer with nearly two decades in IT systems administration, software development, and team leadership. I specialize in Windows Server environments, VMware vSphere infrastructure, and Linux systems, and I rely on scripting to automate the repetitive work so the team can focus on what actually needs a human.

> Core competencies: systems engineering and administration, VMware vSphere virtualization, OS hardening, automation scripting, advanced troubleshooting, TCP/IP networking, and Active Directory administration. Day to day, that means PowerShell, PowerCLI, VMware vCenter, Git, Ansible, and Bash — with my current role centered heavily on Microsoft Windows environments.

## Professional Background

I'm multilingual, fluent in English and French, with a computer science background that gave me a solid foundation in programming, system design, and software engineering principles — the same fundamentals I still lean on today, whether I'm debugging a script or explaining a technical trade-off to someone who doesn't want the technical detail.

## Career Experience

My career has spanned software development, systems engineering, and senior systems administration, most of it in cross-functional teams solving problems that didn't fit neatly into one discipline. As a team lead, a good part of that work is translation in both directions: distilling technical risk and status into something an executive can act on without a briefing document, and turning that same context into something a junior admin can actually learn from rather than just execute. I care about that second part in particular — the colleagues I've mentored are the work I'm proudest of, more than any single system I've worked on.

## Personal Interests

I stay curious about emerging technology and make a point of continuing to learn outside of work, not just when a project demands it. Beyond that, I read regularly and spend time outdoors — walking clears my head better than almost anything else, and it's usually where my best troubleshooting ideas show up uninvited.

## Professional Philosophy

Technical depth matters, but on its own it doesn't ship anything or grow anyone. What I've tried to build over the years is the combination: solid engineering judgment, the ability to lead a team through ambiguity, and enough patience to explain the "why" so it sticks. That's the version of this job I find worth doing — solving the problem in front of me while leaving the team, and whoever inherits the system next, better off than they started.
